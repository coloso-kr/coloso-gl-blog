## 💡 Nanigashi's Core Principles for Captivating Action

### 1. The "Speed of Movement" Mindset
It's not just about how fast your character moves; it's about how you *convey* that speed. Nanigashi emphasizes understanding how to manipulate basic shapes to communicate velocity. This foundational knowledge is crucial for creating impactful actions, whether it's a simple loop or a complex fight sequence. It's not just about learning the technique — it's about shifting how you think about conveying motion itself.

### 2. Efficiency Through Strategic Limited Animation
Mastering limited animation isn't about cutting corners; it's about intentionality. Learn to leverage fewer frames to create a distinct, stylized look that can be incredibly effective. Nanigashi's workflow highlights how to achieve this with practical exercises, focusing on core movements and impactful poses to convey character and action efficiently. This is where strategic workflow choices dramatically cut down production time.

### 3. The "Playfulness" Finishing Touch
Elevating your animation from functional to captivating often comes down to subtle details that inject personality. Nanigashi shares insights on how to add "playfulness" to character motions through exaggeration and intentional imperfection. This one tweak—focusing on how characters move and react with personality—can seriously level up your output, making even simple actions memorable.
