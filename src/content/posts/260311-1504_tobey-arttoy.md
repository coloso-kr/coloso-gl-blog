## 💡 Tobey's Top 3 Tips for Polished Art Toys

### 1. Prioritize Form and Functionality from the Start

The secret to a successful art toy isn't just aesthetics; it's how the parts will assemble and articulate. When sculpting in ZBrush, think about **division points** and **joint mechanisms** early on. Tobey emphasizes creating parts that are not only visually appealing but also practical for printing and assembly. This means considering tolerances, connection types (like pegs and sockets), and how the final toy will hold poses. It's not just about learning a technique—it's about shifting your mindset from a purely digital sculpture to a physical product design.

### 2. Streamline Your Workflow with Custom ZBrush UI

Time is a creator's most valuable asset. Tobey shares his custom UI setup and shortcut configurations, honed over years of professional practice. Implementing these small workflow tweaks can drastically **reduce repetitive actions** and speed up your modeling process. Instead of hunting for tools, you’ll have them readily accessible. Most pros do it this way: optimize your workspace so the software serves your creative vision, not the other way around.

### 3. The "Almost Done" Polish: Master Post-Processing Techniques

The difference between a good print and a professional-looking art toy often lies in the post-processing. This includes **sanding, applying surfacers, and precise painting**. Tobey walks through each stage, showing how to achieve smooth surfaces, sharp color transitions, and realistic finishes. This one tweak—dedicating time to meticulous post-processing—can seriously level up your output quality, making your digital sculpt truly shine in the real world.
